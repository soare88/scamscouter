Common phishing risk signals

  • Unknown link or unusual domain extension.
  • Request for payment, card data, code or password.
  • Urgent warning, threat of account suspension, or offer that sounds too good.
  • Known brand copied in an unknown link.

How ScamScouter helps

ScamScouter checks text, links, domains, emails and text extracted from screenshots. The result gives an instant risk score and clear explanations to protect you from fraud.

Quick safety tips

  • Do not enter card details, passwords or verification codes through links received in messages.
  • Open the official domain manually instead of using the link you received.
  • If the message asks for money or creates urgency, treat it with extreme caution.
