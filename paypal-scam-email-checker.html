Is This PayPal Email Real? How to Tell the Difference

Real PayPal emails always come from an address ending in @paypal.com. They address you by your full name, not "Dear Customer". And they never ask you to verify your password, card number or bank account details by clicking a link in an email.

Most Common PayPal Scam Email Types

  • Account suspension emails: Create urgency claiming your account will be closed in 24 hours.
  • Fake invoice emails: Sent through PayPal's own system but for fake services, asking you to call a scam support number.
  • Overpayment scams: A buyer sends more than the agreed amount and asks you to refund the difference before the original payment is reversed.

What to Do if You Clicked a Link

If you entered your PayPal password on a fake page, go to paypal.com directly and change your password immediately. Enable two-factor authentication from your account security settings. If you entered card details, contact your bank.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I know if a PayPal email is real or fake?

Check the sender address — it must end in @paypal.com exactly. Check that your full name is used in the greeting.

What is the PayPal invoice scam?

Scammers use PayPal's real invoice system to send fraudulent payment requests, bypassing spam filters.
