How to use these scam guides

If you received a suspicious message, email, social media request or payment link, start by identifying the type of scam. Then open the matching ScamScouter guide and compare the message with the warning signs listed there.

  • Check the exact website domain before entering personal details.
  • Do not share card data, passwords or SMS verification codes through links received unexpectedly.
  • Be careful with urgent messages, fake delivery fees, fake investment promises and marketplace payment links.
  • When in doubt, use ScamScouter before clicking, paying or sending information.
