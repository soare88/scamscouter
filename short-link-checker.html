Why short links need caution

A short link can redirect to a phishing page, fake store, malware download or payment scam. You cannot judge it safely from the shortened URL alone.

Common places short link scams appear

  • Delivery fee texts and fake courier alerts.
  • Bank, postal or tax alerts.
  • WhatsApp and social media direct messages.
  • Fake giveaways and crypto promotions.
  • Marketplace payment links.

What to check

Look at the message context, the sender, the final domain and any request for payment or personal information. When in doubt, scan it first.
