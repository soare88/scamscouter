Common risk signals

  • Unknown link or unusual domain claiming to be a government tax portal.
  • Request for payment, card data, SMS code or passwords via email or text.
  • Urgent warning about unpaid taxes or a threat of legal action.
  • Promises of unexpected tax refunds that require you to "claim" them.

Quick safety tips

  • Do not enter card details, passwords or personal identification numbers through links received in messages.
  • Open the official government tax portal manually instead of using the link you received.
  • If the message asks for money, crypto, or gift cards, it is a scam. Treat it with extreme caution.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do tax agencies send refund links by text or email?

No. Official tax agencies generally do not initiate contact with taxpayers by email, text messages, or social media to request personal or financial information.

How do I verify a tax notice?

Log in directly to the official government tax portal manually, without using any links provided in the message.

What if I clicked a fake tax link?

Do not enter any details. If you already submitted personal data or card info, monitor your bank account and contact your local authorities immediately.
