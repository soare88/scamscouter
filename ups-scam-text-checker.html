Common risk signals

  • Unknown link or an unusual domain posing as UPS tracking.
  • Requests for small payments, card data, or personal passwords.
  • Urgent warnings about failed delivery attempts or returned packages.
  • Generic greetings lacking your actual name or correct tracking number.

How ScamScouter helps

ScamScouter checks text, links, domains, emails and text extracted from screenshots. The result gives a clear risk score and practical explanations.

Quick safety tips

  • Do not enter card details or passwords through links received in random text messages.
  • Open the official UPS website manually in your browser instead of clicking the provided link.
  • If the message asks for money or creates false urgency, treat it with caution.

Frequently Asked Questions

Does UPS send text messages with links?

UPS may send tracking updates if you opted in, but they will never ask for unexpected personal information or payment details via an unsolicited text link.

How do I check a suspicious UPS tracking link?

Do not click it. Copy the link and paste it into ScamScouter, or simply go to UPS.com manually and enter your tracking number.

What if I already paid a fake UPS delivery fee?

Contact your bank or credit card provider immediately to report the fraud, block the card, and request a chargeback.
