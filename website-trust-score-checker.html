What a website trust score should consider

A single visual impression is not enough. Scam websites can look professional, use HTTPS and display fake reviews. A better trust check looks at the domain, context, payment behavior, contact details and pressure tactics.

Risk signals ScamScouter looks for

  • Unusual domains, extra words, hyphens or brand impersonation.
  • Requests for card details, passwords or verification codes.
  • Fake checkout pages or suspicious payment instructions.
  • Urgent messages, threats or unrealistic offers.
  • Missing company details, unclear return policy or copied legal pages.

When to check a website trust score

  • Before buying from a store you have never used.
  • Before entering card details on a payment page.
  • After receiving a link by SMS, email or WhatsApp.
  • When a website claims to represent a known brand but the domain looks different.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can a trust score guarantee that a website is safe?

No. A trust score is a risk analysis, not a 100% guarantee. Use it together with your own verification and official sources.

Can a fake website use HTTPS?

Yes. HTTPS only means the connection is encrypted. It does not prove that the website or company is legitimate.

What should I do if a website has a low trust score?

Do not enter sensitive data. Verify the domain, company details, reviews and payment method before taking action.
