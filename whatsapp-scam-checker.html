Common WhatsApp scam patterns

WhatsApp scams often use urgency and trust. A fake buyer may send a payment link, a compromised contact may ask for money, or someone may request a verification code that can take over your account.

Examples of suspicious WhatsApp messages

  • “I changed my number. Can you send money urgently?”
  • “I paid through a courier link. Enter your card to receive the money.”
  • “You received a code by mistake. Send it to me quickly.”
  • “This investment opportunity is only open today.”

Warning signs to check

  • The person refuses a voice or video call.
  • The message asks for a verification code, card details or money.
  • The link leads to an unknown payment or courier page.
  • The message creates pressure to act immediately.
  • The writing style does not match the real person.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can a WhatsApp account be used for scams?

Yes. Scammers can use fake profiles or compromised real accounts to send urgent messages, payment links or code requests.

Should I send a verification code on WhatsApp?

No. Verification codes can be used to take over accounts or confirm actions you did not start.

How do I check a WhatsApp link?

Copy the link or message and paste it into ScamScouter before opening it or entering any data.
